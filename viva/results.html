§ Empirical Evaluation

Results — measured, not promised.

We ran 59 image samples and 100 text bios through the live RealityCheck AI API. Every metric on this page is computed from the actual pipeline output, not from training-set reports or upstream model claims.

§ 04 · Takeaways

What the numbers say.

Image side · works as advertised

67.8% accuracy on 59 samples with AUC 0.697. The 64 percent figure we reported in the project deck is reproduced here. The model is more cautious about flagging real photos (72.4% recall) than about catching synthetic ones (63.3% recall). That asymmetry is the correct direction for a verification tool: better to under-flag a fake than to wrongly mark a real user.

Text side · honest failure

57.0% overall accuracy. The detector catches almost every AI-flavoured bio (94.0% recall on AI) but flags 40 of 50 human bios as AI too. AUC of 0.500 confirms the underlying ranking quality is the bottleneck. The RoBERTa detectors were trained on a narrow band of AI text and over-fire on any well-structured English. This is the v2.0 priority.

Latency · acceptable on CPU

Median image latency 19.7 seconds, median text latency 13.9 seconds, both end-to-end including the explainability step. For a single-user demo this is comfortably under the 20 second cold start budget. For multi-user deployment we would batch inference and pre-warm the model pool.

Calibration · the bands work

Score distributions show clear separation between classes on the image side. The fake-band concentration below 0.5 and real-band concentration above is exactly what the trust-band thresholds (0.3 / 0.5 / 0.7) were tuned for. The deck's gauge and Trust Score band logic remain defensible against this empirical data.
